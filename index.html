The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus, the renowned social entrepreneur and Nobel Peace Prize laureate, has long been an advocate for microfinance and social business as a means to alleviate poverty. Through the establishment of Grameen Bank in 1983, he revolutionized the concept of providing financial services to the impoverished, empowering millions of individuals, especially women, to rise above poverty. His vision for a world where financial access is not dictated by wealth, but by potential, has laid the foundation for Bangladesh's economic transformation. As Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh, Dr. Yunus continued his mission, emphasizing sustainable development, entrepreneurship, and social innovation as the pillars for the nation's future. His leadership in fostering inclusive economic growth aligns with the goals of Bangladesh 2.0—creating a new, prosperous Bangladesh grounded in social justice, innovation, and equality.
